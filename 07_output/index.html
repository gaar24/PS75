Week 7:Electronic Output Devices

Assignment

For this weeks assignment we had to use an output device that I havent used and write a microcontroller program that integrates at least one input device and one output device. Then, use an oscilliscioe to discover the time domain at which the output device is operating.

Materials Used

USB, Potentiometer ( capable of regulating the intensity of the current that circuates through the circuit9, pushbutton, buzzer, breadboard, wires, arduino program

Music Player

I decided to build a program capable of playing " Happy Birthday" throughout the buzzer. For that, I started by playing with other output devices learned in class, such as the RGB LEDs, LED strip. Then, I started to make my music player that when you press the pushbutton, Happy Birthday song will start playing and it can be regulated with a potentiometer. Before this, I actually had to learn how to use the buzzerm and it wasnt that hard and so due to the fact that the buzzer would not stop making noises, I decided to add a pushbutton. To do this, I put the potentiometer in the center of the breadboard so that I could connect pin 12 to the middle lead and the other two leads to the speaker. Next, I add a pusbutton. I coonected one lead with pin 3 and the other to GND. Finally, I connected one lead to GND of the arduino board. I then made sure that all the connections were good to go abd connected the hardware to the software. For the code, I decided to do it by myself and basically started by declaring variables for the musical notes of my song. Before starting I added "int" which basically means integer and is the data type for number storage. Then, for my first note which was g4, i added its correpsonding frequency that took from a table, in this case, 392. I then did the same with all the notes. Afterwards, I declared the variables for duration measured in milliseconds, and "void setup". Lastly, I added the whole code of my song into the void loop space.
